# Investment Memo — LedgerLite

**Date:** July 2025
**Sector:** Fintech
**Stage:** Series A ($12M round)
**Author:** Investment team
**Recommendation: INVEST ($3M)**

## Company overview
LedgerLite is an embedded accounting and reconciliation API for vertical SaaS platforms. Instead of integrating QuickBooks, a vertical SaaS company (e.g. a salon-management or trucking platform) drops in LedgerLite to give its customers built-in bookkeeping, invoicing, and bank reconciliation.

## Founders
- **Sofia Reyes** — CEO. Former product lead at a major payments company; deep payments and ledger expertise.
- **Tom Blackwell** — COO. Scaled operations at a high-growth fintech from Series A to C.
- **Arjun Mehta** — CTO. Built double-entry ledger systems at two prior fintechs.

Exceptional, complementary team with direct experience building exactly this.

## Problem & solution
Vertical SaaS platforms want to own the financial layer (it's sticky and high-margin) but building a correct, auditable double-entry ledger is genuinely hard and off-mission. LedgerLite provides it as an API with a white-label UI. Their ledger is provably correct and supports multi-entity, multi-currency.

## Traction
- $1.8M ARR, growing 18% month-over-month for the last 6 months.
- 22 platform customers, including two that each contribute >$200k ARR.
- Net revenue retention of 134%.
- Gross margin ~82%.

## Business model
Platform fee + per-active-end-user pricing + a small take rate on payment volume reconciled. Revenue compounds as customers' own customers grow.

## Market
Embedded fintech is a large secular wave. The reconciliation/accounting slice is underserved relative to payments. Comparable embedded-finance companies have reached significant scale.

## Strengths
- Best-in-class team for this specific problem.
- Strong, efficient growth with excellent retention (134% NRR).
- Real moat: correctness + auditability + switching costs once a platform embeds the ledger.
- Healthy margins and capital efficiency.

## Risks
- Concentration: top two customers are a meaningful share of ARR.
- Embedded finance can attract regulatory scrutiny as volume grows.
- Competitive entrants from larger payments incumbents.

## Decision
**INVEST.** This is the rare combination of an A+ team, a hard technical moat, and efficient compounding growth in a large market. The customer concentration is the main watch-item, but the pipeline derisks it. Leading the $3M allocation in the Series A.
