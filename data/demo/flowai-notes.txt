FlowAI — Founder meeting notes
Met: October 2025
Sector: SaaS (AI workflow automation)
Stage: Seed
Status: TRACKING (not investing now, stay close)

Who we met:
- Grace Lin, CEO. Ex-product manager at a big workflow/automation company. Sharp on GTM, very fast.
- Raj Patel, CTO. Solid engineer, built the v1 mostly solo.

What they do:
FlowAI is "Zapier with an AI brain." Instead of building automations step by step, you describe the outcome in plain English ("when a new enterprise lead comes in, enrich it, score it, and route hot ones to the right rep on Slack") and FlowAI assembles and runs the workflow. Connects to ~80 SaaS apps today.

Why it's interesting:
- The natural-language-to-workflow demo is slick and clearly resonates with non-technical ops people.
- Time-to-first-automation is minutes vs hours for traditional tools.
- Riding the same wave as a lot of "AI agent" enthusiasm, but with a concrete, boring, useful job-to-be-done.

Traction:
- ~$9k MRR, mostly $29-49/mo prosumer plans.
- 1,300 signups, ~250 activated (built at least one live automation).
- Growth is okay but not explosive: ~10% MoM.

Concerns (why we're tracking, not investing):
- PRICING: $29-49/mo is too low for the value and too high for hobbyists — stuck in the middle. No clear path to expansion revenue yet. They haven't figured out seat/usage pricing.
- RETENTION: month-2 retention around 55%. People build one automation and drift. Needs to become a system-of-record habit.
- Reliability: when an automation silently fails, trust evaporates. Error handling / observability is thin.
- Crowded "AI automation" space; differentiation today is UX, which is copyable.

What would make us lean in:
- Move upmarket to teams with proper pricing and show net revenue retention > 110%.
- Demonstrate month-3 retention improving materially.
- A reliability/observability story so automations are trustworthy.

Next step: check back in one to two quarters. Intro'd them to two ops leaders in our network for design feedback.
