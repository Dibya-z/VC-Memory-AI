# DevAI — Seed Pitch Deck

**Tagline:** The AI engineering teammate for software teams — not just autocomplete, a colleague that ships.

## The problem
Engineering teams are drowning in maintenance work: bug triage, dependency upgrades, test writing, code review, and small feature tickets. Senior engineers spend their time on low-leverage work instead of hard problems. Existing AI coding tools help individuals autocomplete faster, but they don't operate at the level of a team's backlog.

## The solution
DevAI is an AI engineering teammate that connects to your GitHub, Linear, and CI. Assign it a ticket like you'd assign a junior engineer; it opens a branch, writes the code and tests, runs CI, and submits a PR for human review. It learns the team's conventions from the existing codebase and past PRs.

## Why now
- Agentic coding models crossed the threshold where multi-file, test-backed PRs are reliable for well-scoped tickets.
- Teams are under pressure to ship more without growing headcount.

## Traction
- **14 paying teams** (not individuals) on annual contracts, $18k–40k ACV.
- **$310k committed ARR**, up from $90k two quarters ago.
- Net revenue retention 128% — teams expand seats after the first month.
- Median team merges ~30 DevAI-authored PRs/week; **logo retention 100% so far** (no paying team has churned).

## Business model
Per-seat annual subscription for the team plus a usage component for agent runs. Lands with a team lead, expands across engineering.

## Market
The AI developer-tools category is large and fast-growing, but DevAI targets the team/enterprise budget (CI, code review, backlog throughput) rather than the individual autocomplete seat.

## Competition
GitHub Copilot, Cursor, and several agentic-coding startups. DevAI's wedge is operating on the team's actual backlog and CI, not the editor — and integrating where work is tracked (Linear/Jira) rather than only where code is typed.

## What the team flags as risks
- Trust: teams must be confident an agent-authored PR is safe to merge; the human-review gate is essential.
- Competition from incumbents who own the editor and the repo.
