# Cascade — Seed Pitch Deck

**Tagline:** Describe the outcome, we build the workflow — AI automation for operations teams.

## The problem
Ops teams stitch together repetitive cross-app processes by hand. Traditional automation tools (Zapier-style) still make you build every step manually, which is slow and brittle for non-technical users.

## The solution
Cascade is "automation with an AI brain." Describe the outcome in plain English — "when a new enterprise lead comes in, enrich it, score it, and route hot ones to the right rep in Slack" — and Cascade assembles and runs the workflow. It connects to ~90 SaaS apps today.

## Why now
- LLMs make natural-language-to-workflow genuinely usable for non-technical operators.
- Every team wants to do more without adding headcount.

## Traction
- ~$11k MRR, mostly on $29–59/month prosumer plans.
- 1,500 signups, ~280 activated (built at least one live automation).
- Growth around 10% month-over-month.

## Founders
- **Maya Fischer** — CEO. Former PM at a large workflow-automation company; strong on GTM.
- **Sanjay Iyer** — CTO. Built the v1 largely solo; solid engineer.

## Strengths
- Slick natural-language demo that clearly resonates with ops people.
- Time-to-first-automation is minutes versus hours for legacy tools.

## Risks the team acknowledges
- Pricing is stuck in the middle — too high for hobbyists, too low to capture the value; no clear expansion-revenue path yet.
- Month-2 retention is around 55%: people build one automation and drift.
- Reliability matters — when an automation silently fails, trust evaporates.
- Crowded "AI automation" space; current edge is UX, which is copyable.

## The ask
Raising $3.5M seed to move upmarket to teams, fix pricing, and harden reliability.
