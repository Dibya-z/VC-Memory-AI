# Investment Memo — NimbusHealth

**Date:** December 2025
**Sector:** Healthcare
**Stage:** Series A ($14M round)
**Author:** Investment team
**Recommendation: INTERESTED — needs partner review**

## Company overview
NimbusHealth builds an ambient AI medical scribe for outpatient clinics. It listens to the doctor–patient conversation (with consent), then drafts the clinical note, suggests billing codes, and pushes a structured summary into the EHR. The goal is to give physicians back the ~2 hours/day they lose to documentation.

## Founders
- **Dr. Elena Vasquez** — CEO. Practicing internist for 9 years; lived the documentation-burnout problem firsthand. Left clinical practice to start NimbusHealth.
- **Kwame Boateng** — CTO. Former speech-recognition lead at a large voice-AI company; deep ASR and medical-NLP background.
- **Rachel Stern** — VP Sales. Sold EHR-adjacent software to health systems for a decade.

Unusually strong founder-market fit: a physician who felt the pain paired with a genuine speech-AI expert.

## Problem & solution
Physician burnout is at crisis levels, and documentation is the single biggest driver. Existing scribe solutions are either expensive human scribes or clunky dictation tools. NimbusHealth is fully ambient — no templates, no dictation commands — and integrates directly with the two leading EHRs.

## Traction
- $2.1M ARR, growing ~15% month-over-month for the last 5 months.
- 38 clinics live; 1,100 physicians using it weekly.
- Net revenue retention 121%; gross margin 71% and improving as inference costs fall.
- Average physician saves a self-reported 90 minutes/day; clinic-level renewal rate 94%.

## Business model
Per-physician monthly subscription ($299–399/seat) sold to clinic groups, with an enterprise tier for health systems. Expansion happens clinic-by-clinic within a group.

## Market
Ambient clinical documentation is one of the fastest-moving healthcare-AI categories. The broader clinical-documentation market is very large, and reimbursement tailwinds favor tools that improve coding accuracy.

## Strengths
- Exceptional founder-market fit and a credible speech-AI team.
- Strong, efficient growth with high retention (121% NRR, 94% clinic renewal).
- Clear, measurable ROI (time saved + cleaner coding) that clinics feel immediately.

## Risks & concerns
- **Regulatory & liability:** AI-drafted clinical notes carry malpractice and compliance exposure; the human-review step must be airtight.
- **Competition:** several well-funded entrants and EHR incumbents are racing into ambient scribing.
- **Data/privacy:** handling PHI raises the security and HIPAA bar significantly.
- **Model cost & accuracy** in noisy clinical environments needs continued investment.

## Open questions for the founders
- How defensible is the EHR integration as incumbents add native scribing?
- What is month-6+ physician retention (not just clinic renewal)?
- What is the plan for malpractice/liability indemnification?

## Decision
**INTERESTED — needs partner review.** This has the markers we like: rare founder-market fit, efficient growth, and measurable ROI in a large market. The regulatory/liability and competitive-moat questions are real and warrant deeper diligence before we'd commit. Recommend a partner meeting and a reference call with two live clinics.
